## Core Business Model To Enforce

Plug-A-Pro is a matching platform between:

- customers with real-world service problems
- independent service providers, many of whom may be informal workers

Plug-A-Pro helps both sides:

- discover each other
- share request detail
- view provider profile and trust signals
- clarify scope
- issue and accept quotes
- coordinate the job
- keep a written trace of key interactions
- complete the transaction

Plug-A-Pro is not, by default:

- the employer of the provider
- the guarantor of provider safety
- the guarantor of provider character
- the guarantor of legality or licensing
- the guarantor of workmanship
- a blanket certification authority

The platform may record, facilitate, or review certain things, but it must not imply stronger verification or responsibility than it can actually support.

## Persona Lens To Apply

The AI must evaluate every piece of copy against these role groups:

### 1. Platform Role

The content must support operational honesty, risk clarity, and realistic escalation expectations.

### 2. Service Provider Role

The content must speak credibly to:

- solo operators
- small team owners
- informal or emerging providers
- structured SMEs

It must not assume every provider is a formal company with certifications and polished back-office systems.

### 3. Client / Service Requester Role

The content must work for:

- household clients
- busy working professionals
- family decision-makers
- tenants with urgent issues
- small business owners
- property managers
- trust-sensitive first-time customers

The copy must reduce uncertainty, explain the journey clearly, and support informed choice.

## Classification Framework

For each claim or copy block, classify it as:

### Accurate

The wording is supported by real product or operational capability.

### Overstated

The direction is partly true, but the wording is stronger than the underlying reality.

### Misleading

The wording is likely to make users believe Plug-A-Pro has vetted, guaranteed, or enforced something it has not.

### Unsupported

No meaningful implementation or operational process supports the claim.

## Rewrite Rules

When rewriting:

- preserve trust
- preserve clarity
- preserve conversion value
- remove fake assurance
- prefer factual, narrow wording
- explain the customer decision process honestly
- reflect the informal-service-provider reality
- make the platform feel structured and helpful without pretending it eliminates all risk

### Preferred replacement patterns

Use language such as:

- `independent provider`
- `provider profile`
- `provider-supplied details`
- `provider-shared evidence`
- `marketplace profile reviewed for participation`
- `platform-recorded job history`
- `reviews collected on Plug-A-Pro`
- `quote, booking, and job details recorded in writing`
- `compare the profile, evidence, and quote before proceeding`

Avoid umbrella trust labels unless the underlying verification is genuinely implemented.

## Persona-Specific Copy Standards

### Customer-Facing Copy Must

- begin from a real service problem
- explain how the platform helps the customer evaluate who they are dealing with
- show that the customer should review provider profile, quote, and evidence before proceeding
- reduce fear without pretending the platform guarantees the provider
- be practical and mobile-first
- work well in WhatsApp and short-form reading contexts

### Provider-Facing Copy Must

- speak to informal and emerging providers credibly
- avoid assuming they are already formal businesses
- explain what they can submit, show, and improve over time
- not imply they have been publicly certified unless they truly have
- make participation feel accessible without lowering honesty

### Policy Copy Must

- define clearly what Plug-A-Pro facilitates, records, and supports
- define clearly what Plug-A-Pro does not guarantee
- avoid hidden overclaims in legal or support wording
- align with actual dispute, payment, cancellation, and complaint capabilities

### WhatsApp Copy Must

- be short, clear, action-oriented
- align with the role and stage in the journey
- use WhatsApp for reassurance and prompts
- avoid making the bot sound like Plug-A-Pro has stronger operational control than it really has
- support quote review, booking clarity, ETA confidence, payment clarity, and issue reporting

State whether the current content:

- reflects a transparent matching platform
- over-claims responsibility

### 5. Escalation List

Flag anything that needs human decision because it touches:

- legal liability
- safety claims

- insurance or licensing language

## Output Style Requirements

The AI's output must:

- be specific to Plug-A-Pro's marketplace model

- separate customer-facing, provider-facing, and policy-facing issues
- identify both copy risk and missing trust scaffolding
- preserve conversion value while increasing honesty
