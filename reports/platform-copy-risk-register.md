# Plug A Pro Copy Risk Register

Date: 2026-04-10

## 1. Purpose

This register captures user-facing claims that were reviewed for implied platform responsibility, provider vetting, or trust overstatement.

Classifications:

- `Accurate`
- `Overstated`
- `Misleading`
- `Unsupported`

## 2. Copy Risk Register

| Claim or label | Location | Pre-fix classification | Why it was risky | Fix applied | Residual decision |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| “vetted local worker” | marketing hero | Unsupported | No implemented vetting programme supported this claim | Rewritten to honest matching language | Keep banned unless real programme exists |
| “trusted home services” | field-service customer home | Overstated | Suggested stronger provider assurance than implemented | Rewritten to neutral/home-service marketplace framing | None |
| “Fast. Reliable. Guaranteed.” | field-service customer home | Unsupported | Suggested outcome guarantee and provider reliability warranty | Removed / softened | Do not reuse unless guarantee terms exist |

## 3. Corrected Claim Set

The following phrasing direction is now the preferred pattern:

- `application reviewed for marketplace participation`
- `provider-supplied profile details`
- `platform-recorded completed jobs, quotes, and reviews`
- `Plug A Pro does not claim licensing, background checks, or workmanship guarantees unless a specific field says so`
- `risk-reduction through records, traceability, and customer approvals`

## 4. Residual Legal / Product Decisions Requiring Human Input

1. Will Plug A Pro ever operate a real verification programme?
2. If yes, which checks are in scope:
   - identity
   - licence
   - qualification
   - references
   - banking
3. Which payment paths are genuinely platform-controlled versus merely recorded?
4. What refund commitments is the business prepared to operationally honour for:
   - online payments
   - offline EFT
   - cash or direct payments
5. Should certain service categories require stronger onboarding or category-specific evidence before being listed?

## 5. Copy Governance Recommendation

Before launch and before future marketing pushes, Plug A Pro should maintain a blocked-terms list unless a documented process supports each claim.

Blocked umbrella terms by default:

- verified
- vetted
- trusted professional
- qualified
- certified
- approved professional
- safe
- guaranteed
- protected

Allowed only when narrowed and evidenced:

- `identity document reviewed on [date]`
- `trade certificate uploaded`
- `licence reviewed`
- `platform-recorded completed jobs`
- `customer reviews collected through Plug A Pro`
